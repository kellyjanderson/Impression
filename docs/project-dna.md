# Impression Project DNA

This is the quality bar for the entire project.

## Founding Sentiment (Preserved)

> Not just teach but impress. Loft satisifies many difficlut modling problems, and bridges the mechanical organic devide. We should be thouhtfull, and put real effort in to making examples that allow people to dream big about what Impression can do for them. Examples are both marketing and teaching tools. They are the first thing that people will see Impression do and they should Impress. The name of our product gives us big shoes to fill so we should always look to be Impressive.

## Core Value: Impress Through Utility

Impression is not just a modeling toolkit that works. It is a toolkit that should expand what users believe they can build.

Every feature, example, and workflow should:

- solve real modeling problems, not just expose raw functionality
- bridge mechanical precision and organic form in a coherent way
- feel thoughtful, polished, and intentionally designed
- help users dream bigger about what Impression enables

Correctness is required. Clarity is required. But our standard is higher than correctness alone: the result should be genuinely impressive.

## Product Consequences

This value applies to all areas, not only examples:

- API design: ergonomic, consistent, and expressive
- geometry behavior: robust, deterministic, and trustworthy
- documentation: purpose-first, practical, and inspiring
- examples: contextual, ambitious, and visually clear
- tooling and UX: fast feedback, low friction, and confidence-building

## Decision Filter

When choosing between alternatives, prefer the option that best combines:

1. Real-world usefulness
2. Technical rigor
3. User delight and ambition

If an output is technically correct but fails to communicate clear utility or potential, it is not complete.
