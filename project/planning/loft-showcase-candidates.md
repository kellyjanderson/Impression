# Loft Showcase Candidates

## Goal

Find one real-world object that is:

- difficult to model well without a strong loft tool
- useful and believable as an engineering object
- visually compelling enough to feel like an artwork
- precise enough to justify a specification-style modeling plan
- predominantly loft-driven rather than a pile of unrelated operations

## Strong Candidates

### 1. High-Performance Cyclist Helmet Shell

Why it is hard:

- outer shell changes from blunt forehead to elongated tail
- vents appear, split, merge, widen, and taper
- interior void and exterior skin both evolve
- aesthetic fairness matters as much as raw geometry

Why it is beautiful:

- strong silhouette
- clear aerodynamic intent
- visible rhythm from vent topology and shell sweep

Why it is good for loft:

- continuous body with repeated hole evolution
- patch fairness and correspondence both matter
- outer and inner shell can be planned systematically

### 2. Running Shoe Midsole and Heel Cage

Why it is hard:

- cross-sections vary dramatically through the arch, heel, and forefoot
- perforations and relief voids can appear and disappear
- asymmetry is meaningful, not accidental

Why it is beautiful:

- sculptural consumer-product form
- strong balance between softness and structure

Why it is good for loft:

- many controlled section transitions
- real need for correspondence discipline
- easy to make ugly if loft planning is weak

### 3. Formula-Style Front Wing Endplate and Duct Transition

Why it is hard:

- multiple aerodynamic channels interact
- planform shifts while internal passages remain controlled
- edges need both precision and fairness

Why it is beautiful:

- sharp, technical, high-performance look
- obvious functional geometry

Why it is good for loft:

- multi-region and multi-hole transitions
- demanding section control
- convincing proof of engineering-grade lofting

### 4. Prosthetic Socket with Vent and Relief Topology

Why it is hard:

- organic but precise shape
- local relief zones and vent apertures change along progression
- comfort-driven geometry means fairness cannot be sloppy

Why it is beautiful:

- human-centered object
- medical/product design language can be elegant and calm

Why it is good for loft:

- sections carry real-world meaning
- hole placement and wall flow matter
- ideal for “functional precision with organic form”

### 5. Ergonomic Power-Tool Grip with Internal Skeleton

Why it is hard:

- exterior grip shape and internal structure evolve differently
- attachment, finger relief, and cable/battery transitions complicate topology

Why it is beautiful:

- expressive consumer-product silhouette
- tactile, muscular geometry

Why it is good for loft:

- mixed outer and inner topology
- asymmetric correspondence
- good test of practical modeling value

### 6. Bifurcated Intake Manifold or HVAC Plenum

Why it is hard:

- branch split/merge is the core problem
- duct sections change from round to rectangular and back
- internal flow geometry must remain believable

Why it is beautiful:

- clean technical form
- strong “all loft” identity

Why it is good for loft:

- split/merge is central
- easy to justify with engineering reality
- already close to our existing strengths

### 7. Wearable Smart Glasses Temple and Hinge Fairing

Why it is hard:

- thin, highly controlled cross-sections
- strong asymmetry and tight industrial-design tolerances
- transitions between lens rim, hinge housing, and temple arm are subtle

Why it is beautiful:

- premium consumer-electronics feel
- elegant proportions

Why it is good for loft:

- loft quality is immediately visible
- small correspondence mistakes look terrible

### 8. Performance Bicycle Saddle Shell with Cutout

Why it is hard:

- thin shell fairness is unforgiving
- center cutout evolves strongly
- underside reinforcement and top contour can diverge

Why it is beautiful:

- iconic sculptural profile
- strong engineering/product crossover

Why it is good for loft:

- hole topology matters
- fairness matters
- very clear before/after quality judgment

## Best First Picks

If the goal is to make one truly memorable “all loft” showcase, the strongest first options are:

1. `High-Performance Cyclist Helmet Shell`
2. `Formula-Style Front Wing Endplate and Duct Transition`
3. `Prosthetic Socket with Vent and Relief Topology`

## Recommended First Pick

`High-Performance Cyclist Helmet Shell`

Why:

- it is hard in exactly the right way
- it combines exterior beauty with real topology evolution
- vents give us hole birth/split/merge/death opportunities
- it can be both artistic and obviously functional
- the result is visually impressive even to non-programmers
